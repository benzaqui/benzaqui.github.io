What I believe about AI

How I think about AI leadership, governance, and creating durable value in regulated environments.

The bottleneck has shifted

Building agents is now a commodity. The real challenge is governing them — defining boundaries, maintaining oversight, and ensuring autonomous systems behave as intended at scale. Companies that solve orchestration and governance will pull decisively ahead.

Strategy is subtraction

A list of 300 GenAI ideas is not a strategy. Real AI strategy is ruthless prioritisation — picking the 3 things that create compounding advantage and resourcing them properly. Everything else is distraction.

Value lives after deployment

Most AI projects fail not because the model is wrong, but because the organisation isn't ready for it. Adoption, process redesign, and stakeholder alignment determine ROI. Technical excellence is necessary but never sufficient.

Governance enables ambition

Governance is not a constraint on AI ambition — it is what makes ambition sustainable. Ungoverned agents in production are a liability. The teams that build with governance from day one move faster in the long run, not slower.
